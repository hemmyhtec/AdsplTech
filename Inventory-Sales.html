Forecast Demand in Crisis

Our Various model are trained to Capture the various factors:

When integrating demand forecasting systems, it’s essential to understand that they are vulnerable to anomalies like the COVID-19 pandemic. It means that machine learning models should be upgraded according to a current reality.

As the demand forecasting model processes historical data, it can’t know that the demand has radically changed. For example, if last year, we had one demand indicator for medical face masks and antiviral drugs, this year, it would be completely different.

In that case, there might be several ways to get an accurate forecast:

Collect data about new market behavior.

Once the situation becomes more or less stable, develop a demand forecasting model from scratch.

Apply feature engineering approach.

By processing external data: news, a current market state, price index, exchange rates, and other economic factors, machine learning models are capable of making more up-to-date forecasts.

Upload the most recent POS data.

The period of a loadable dataset might vary from one to two months, depending on the products’ category. In this way, we can timely detect shifts in demand patterns and enhance forecast accuracy.

Apply the transfer learning approach.

If there are any gathered historical data about past pandemics or similar behavior shifts, we can take them and predict demand in the context of the current crisis.

Apply information cascade modeling approach.

Combining the most recent POS data with the cascade modeling, the demand forecasting system can identify herd patterns of human behavior. In other words, we can forecast how people will make buying decisions according to the behavior patterns of most people.

Apply natural language processing (NLP) approach.

NLP technology enables the processing of real comments from social networks, media platforms, and other available social sources. By utilizing text mining and sentiment analysis approaches, NLP models gather samples of customer’s conversations. This method allows the detection of people’s preferences, choices, sentiment, and behavior shifts.

Demand Forecasting - Why it’s needed

Demand forecasting is a method of forecasting inventory values for a future period. It also helps keep track of sales and demand so you can better manage your orders.

Every Businesses & organization works towards improving the demand planning process within the organization. However, after decades of technological advancements and progress, there are currently many solutions to demand planning and forecasting issues.

The negative consequences of poor demand planning includes:

  • Over Stocks
  • Under Stocks
  • Poor Inventory Selection
  • Difficulties in retaining existing customers
  • Difficulties in finding new customers
  • Poor Sales and Operations (S&Ops)
  • Demand Patterns – Right Identification
  • Stock’s mismanagement

Inventory Optimization (Inventory and sales prediction software) – A Must Requirement

Improve inventory accounting, improve your cash flow, and even free up funds for other areas of the business. Always have the right amount of stock, in the right place, at the right time. Never over stock or under stock again.

More sales from less stock- Right time & Right Customers

If you have enough inventory at right time with right prediction, you don’t have to worry about out of stock or reorders. You can fulfil every order immediately upon receipt and deliver the promised delivery to the customers. Channelizing your Inventory level is critical to inventory forecasting as when customers find what they’re looking for online and they get it with right pricing and services, the customer experience remains positive. Businesses loses customers because they don’t have what they need at right time and risk losing their future business.

Less Manual Work – Intelligent Operation

Inventory forecasting tools help automate reordering, predict labor needs on the job site, and account for changes in order of volume, making it easy to understand and reduce inventory costs. This saves time and personnel for warehouse management and all employees.

Efficient production cycle

You can use inventory forecasting to better manage products throughout the retail supply chain. Knowing your manufacturer’s lead times, your warehouse receipt times, and the exact inventory levels for each product you need to place a new order will help you work more efficiently with your supplier and gain a better understanding. You can make informed decisions without having to speed up the production and dispatch of schedules.

Minimize stock levels

Minimum Stock levels mean lost sales. Inventory forecasting can help you avoid this by accurately predicting future demand. With this information, you can better understand when and how many units need to be refilled.

Reduce Costs

Inventory prediction helps with overall inventory management. It helps with inventory management as you only buy what you need and only store those products instead of spending too much on not required.

Reduces wastage of products

Cost reduction in turn reduces the unwanted storage space and the associated costs. Based on this, you can plan to reuse these items or group them with best-selling products. This creates storage space and increases sales.
